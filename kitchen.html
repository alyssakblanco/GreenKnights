Drop the Nonstick Cookware

While the jury is still out on the overall health effects of non-stick cookware, all will agree that with repeated use non-stick coating will degrade and eventually be unusable. This causes a lot of waste and overall cost increase for consumers over time. As most chefs will tell you, a solid cast iron skillet is a kitchen essential. They are built to last generations and the upkeep on them is easier than most think. My pan has been in my family since 1910, and I still use it almost exclusively for many of my meals. Need Some Caffeine? Why Not Also Go Green?

Reusable coffee filters are some of the easiest ways to save money on your morning brew. Why not make some green too? Reusable k-cups are insanely beneficial to the environment. They remove hundreds of plastic containers from landfills, cut down the abundance of aluminum out there, and will save you hundreds of dollars throughout their lifetimes. Machine stopping you from saving the planet, simply take a the lid off a used k-cup and tape it to the inside of the machine. Lets Up That Cutting Board Game

Hardwood and Bamboo cutting boards are great way to look like a green pro in the kitchen. They have naturally antibacterial properties, cause less ware on your knives, and just look amazing. When you're shopping for your new cutting board make sure to look for reclaimed, salvage, or renewable sourced wood. Hardwood by far is the best amongst the rest. Caring for your new cutting board is just as easy as green cleaning your house. White vinegar is a great disinfectant. Simply spray, let it sit, and wipe it off with a warm damp towel. Time to Ditch the Flimsy Plastic Containers

Plastic containers may be cheap and easy to get, but they constantly warp, stain, and melt. Glass is built to last, and contrary to popular opinion, is actually really hard to break. Often built with thick walls to last a lifetime, they make organizing and cutting out plastics extremely easy. Your dorm has limited space, why take it up with bulky plastic containers and boxes, switch to glass today and help save the planet. Check out the apples in my kitchen for more ways to use these containers.

Buy Bulk Whole Foods

Plastic seems impossible to avoid in grocery stores. Now a days everything has plastic wrap. Want to cut down? Buy whole foods. Fruit, nuts, veggies, meat, flour, and spices can all be purchased in bulk minus the plastic. All you have to know is where to go. Its easy, Walmart, publix, and target have large produce sections with plastic free produce. Stores like Whole Foods and Trader Joes have plastic free flours, spices, and nuts. Need some meat sans plastic? Check out your local butcher shop. The Local Butcher in Winter Park is my go to. Its a great place to pick up everything you could want for date night (and they have premarinated stuff if you want impress someone with your "cooking skills" or forgot a special occasion).

Why Not Use Those Scraps

Countertop Composters are a great way to use your food scraps for good. Composting is also a great way to save money with your plants at home. By using a countertop composter you can make your own fertilizer by literally doing nothing. UCF has many areas around campus for composting too, check out our get involved and plants pages to learn more about how composting can help you go green. My favorite drop off location is the Downtown Farmers Market because you can do it before you go shopping for fresh food, and a lot of people let you pet their dogs there.
